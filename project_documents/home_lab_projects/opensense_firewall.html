Proxmox Virtualized Router & Firewall (OPNsense)

Configuring and managing firewall, segmentation with VLANs, and deploying DCHP server.

Overview:

I designed and deployed a virtualized router and firewall solution using Proxmox as the hypervisor and OPNsense as the firewall platform. The objective was to replace traditional consumer-grade routing hardware with a secure, segmented, and scalable virtual infrastructure.

The environment was built on a Dell OptiPlex server equipped with a multi-port Intel NIC. Proxmox was configured as the virtualization layer, with dedicated network bridges for management, WAN, and LAN traffic. OPNsense was deployed as a virtual machine and configured to handle routing, firewall policies, and future services such as DNS (Unbound), DHCP, VPN, and IDS/IPS.

This project simulates a real-world enterprise edge deployment where firewall virtualization, segmentation, and infrastructure resilience are critical.

Complex Challenges Faced:

  • Designing proper network bridge architecture in Proxmox without losing management access
  • Configuring PCI passthrough for WAN interfaces to ensure performance and isolation
  • Troubleshooting UEFI boot issues during OPNsense VM deployment
  • Ensuring proper separation between Proxmox management and firewall WAN interfaces
  • Planning DHCP, DNS, and gateway transitions without interrupting future network expansion

Ethical Considerations and Actions Taken:

  • Ensured firewall management interfaces were never exposed to the WAN
  • Applied the principle of least privilege when designing firewall rule sets
  • Separated management traffic from user traffic to reduce risk exposure
  • Documented configurations clearly to support responsible maintenance and scalability

Outcomes:

  • Successfully deployed a production-ready virtualized firewall environment
  • Established a scalable network foundation capable of supporting VLANs, VPNs, IDS/IPS, and monitoring systems
  • Strengthened skills in virtualization networking, firewall architecture, and infrastructure design
  • Built a reusable enterprise-style network model suitable for business or home lab environments
