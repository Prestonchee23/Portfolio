Hybrid Cloud Backup Proposal

Designed a hybrid backup system using NAS and Google Workspace...

Overview:

I led the design and proposal of a hybrid backup solution for Mathew Made Construction, integrating local NAS storage with Google Workspace cloud services. The goal was to deliver scalable, secure, and cost-effective data protection that supported both offline access to critical files and cloud-based scalability during peak seasonal demands. To meet these objectives, I conducted in-depth technical research, evaluated multiple backup architectures, and developed governance and security policies tailored to the company’s operational needs. The solution emphasized disaster recovery readiness, secure remote access, and budget-conscious implementation.

Complex Challenges Faced:

  • Balancing cost constraints with high availability and data reliability
  • Designing a hybrid architecture that supported both local and cloud-based access
  • Implementing security measures including multi-factor authentication (MFA), VPN access, and encryption
  • Evaluating trade-offs between performance, scalability, and long-term maintenance
  • Aligning technical recommendations with business priorities and seasonal workflows

Ethical Considerations and Actions Taken:

  • Ensured all data handling practices complied with internal security policies and privacy standards
  • Recommended encryption and access control policies to protect sensitive company data
  • Maintained transparency in cost-benefit analysis to support informed decision-making
  • Avoided vendor lock-in by proposing flexible, interoperable solutions

Outcomes:

  • Delivered a comprehensive backup proposal aligned with company goals and budget
  • Strengthened disaster recovery capabilities and data security posture
  • Gained experience in technical evaluation, proposal writing, and stakeholder communication
  • Helped lay the groundwork for scalable infrastructure improvements
